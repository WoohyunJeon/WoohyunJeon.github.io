God I LOVE Accounting

1 September 2023

I often get asked whether it's normal to hate going to work to which I usually go, “Of course, it is”.

If you're 18 and the job you hate is your first job, it's pretty normal. Nobody is handing out super fun gigs to 18-year-olds, and you have to pay your dues. I see a lot of students desperately looking for work and when I suggest they start somewhere small like the service industry (which is always hiring), they scoff at it thinking those jobs are beneath them. That simply won't do. If you have a mouth to feed, you need to stop expecting jobs to be handed to you on a silver platter and give reason to why you're of value. This is why people go down the route of post-secondary education and the trades; it's better to have a handful of diamonds than a pile of sand. Also, if you're (arbitrarily) 45 years old and you loathe going into work every day, I don't know if it's normal, but I can admit that the situation does suck. Most people don't have the luxury of finding their passion and being able to fully pursue it. You won't hear people saying, "God, I LOVE accounting! I can't wait to get to the firm and just open up Excel!".You’ll rarely see anyone like that.

For the sake of brevity, let's call it "work" instead of a job. I hate work. I hate having to do work. I hate having the feeling of work looming over me. I like getting work done; there's a nice feeling I get when I press that submit button. But when I'm actually doing it, the feeling is terrible; I hate it. Obviously, it depends on the type of work. There's a big difference between making a video on YouTube, working on an offshore oil rig in the middle of the ocean, and selling a used Ford F150; there's a significant difference across all these jobs. But I've never clocked into work thinking, "Ooh, work today!" — it's always hate. I hate doing work, but I still trudge through it because that's life. The goal should be to find something you don't hate quite as much as the other things because that's the best you're going to get, and that's the truth most people won't tell you.

Let's say you love playing video games. Try getting a job where you play video games for a living. See how much you like them after that. Do you like having sex? Go join the adult entertainment industry; see how that goes for you. It doesn't work out that way. The folks who make the doughnuts never eat them, and that's just the way life works. If you really want to love your job, then you better make your vocation your vacation; otherwise, it's just not going to happen.
